SYNTHETIC TRAINING DOCUMENT — CT-101 VISIT SCHEDULE

SCREENING — Day -28 to Day -1
Window: must occur within 28 days before Baseline.
Required assessments: informed consent before procedures; demographics; medical and medication history; inclusion and exclusion review; physical examination; vital signs; ECG; pregnancy test when applicable; hematology; chemistry; urinalysis; symptom score; and paper-diary training.

BASELINE — Day 1
Window: no window; procedures occur on Day 1.
Required assessments: confirm continued eligibility; review adverse events and concomitant medication changes; collect fasting laboratory samples; administer four questionnaires; randomize; observe first dose; dispense product; and review diary completion. Planned duration is approximately 4.5 hours.

VISIT 1 — Day 14
Window: plus or minus 2 days (Day 12 through Day 16).
Required assessments: adverse events; concomitant medications; vital signs; symptom score; paper-diary review; adherence; and study-product accountability.

VISIT 2 — Day 28
Window: plus or minus 3 days (Day 25 through Day 31).
Required assessments: adverse events; concomitant medications; vital signs; symptom score; physical examination as indicated; hematology; chemistry; paper-diary review; adherence; and study-product accountability.

VISIT 3 — Day 56
Window: plus or minus 5 days (Day 51 through Day 61).
Required assessments: adverse events; concomitant medications; vital signs; symptom score; hematology; chemistry; paper-diary review; adherence; and study-product accountability.

FINAL TREATMENT — Day 84
Window: plus or minus 5 days (Day 79 through Day 89).
Required assessments: adverse events; concomitant medications; physical examination; vital signs; ECG; hematology; chemistry; urinalysis; symptom and function questionnaires; paper-diary collection; and final product accountability.

FOLLOW-UP — target Day 98
Window: no allowable visit window is defined in Protocol CT-101.
Required assessments: unresolved adverse-event status, new serious adverse events, concomitant medication changes, and confirmation of return or destruction of remaining study materials.
